Senator Edward Kennedy, youngest brother of President Kennedy, was standing near the Senate today when Richard Biddle, a press liaison officer of the Senate, rushed to his desk and whispered to him that his brother had been shot.

The Senator, rushing up his papers and hurried from the chamber, as described by Biddle, Democrat of Florida, took over the Senate's office.

No public announcement was made in the Senate chamber, but Senator Morse, Democrat of Oregon, who was present in the Senate, said, "I am sorry to say that the Senate is in a state of shock and grief."

Senators checked themselves into a nearby cloakroom and gathered around a phone to listen to the Senate's radio.

Many first learned of reports of the shooting from television news added later to the floor.

When he left the Senate, the Senator said, "I don't know what to say about it."

Minority leader Dickerson said that this was the worst catastrophe that could happen to the nation.

Leaders Confer

The Senate met back in session at 2:10 p.m., with valid reasons for the delay. The Senate will not be able to meet until noon.

The two leaders conferred privately as they came back on the floor and said almost nothing to the Senate.

Asked to Pray

The Chaplain said:

"Our Father, the tragic news has come to us here in the Senate, and we are deeply saddened by the news."
